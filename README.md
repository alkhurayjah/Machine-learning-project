# 🦠 Predict the Age of a Trilobite Fossil

This project explores the use of machine learning to estimate the geological age of trilobite fossils. By leveraging biological taxonomy, geographical information, and paleo-environmental features, we aim to build a predictive model capable of accurately classifying fossils into their correct geological time intervals.

## What Were Trilobites?

Trilobites were ancient marine arthropods, related to modern-day insects and crustaceans. They are considered one of the earliest and most successful groups of animals in Earth's history.

- **First Appearance:** ~521 million years ago (Cambrian Period)
- **Extinction:** ~251 million years ago (End-Permian mass extinction)
- **Diversity:** Trilobites occupied a wide range of ecological niches, functioning as predators, scavengers, and filter feeders

## 📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains **29,039 records** of trilobite fossil occurrences, providing valuable insights into life during the Paleozoic Era.

### 🔑 Key Features

- **Taxonomy:** Includes biological classification such as *order, family, genus,* and *species*
- **Geography:** Covers fossil locations including *country, latitude, longitude,* and *formation*
- **Ecology & Biology:** Describes organism characteristics such as *diet, vision type, life habit,* and *environment*
